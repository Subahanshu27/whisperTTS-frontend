How does an AI subtitle generator work?

An AI subtitle generator uses speech recognition to turn the spoken words in a video into text. First, the audio track is extracted from the video. Then an AI model analyses the audio and maps it to text, detecting word boundaries and pauses. Finally, the text is split into timed segments and burned into the video frames. Modern models like Whisper support many languages and handle accents and background noise well.

Frequently asked questions

Is this subtitle generator really free?

Yes. There is no login, no credit card and no watermark on your exported video. It is a demo of what the Floyo API can do.

What video formats and sizes are supported?

MP4 and MOV files up to 100 MB and about 5 minutes long. Trim or compress longer or larger files first.

How accurate are the subtitles?

It depends on audio quality, but the Whisper model typically achieves high accuracy on clear speech.

Which languages can it caption?

Whisper transcribes many spoken languages. Burned-in captions render cleanly for Latin, Cyrillic, Greek, Vietnamese and Chinese scripts, and the right font is selected automatically for the language you choose.

How long can the video be?

Up to about 5 minutes. The captioning workflow loads every frame into memory, so longer clips can exhaust GPU memory and fail.

Can I customise the subtitle style?

Yes. Change the font, size, colour and position, and see a live preview before you render.
